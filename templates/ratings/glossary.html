Glossary of Terms

Understanding the terms used in the Islamic Ethical Gaming Standard (IEGS).

Rating Tiers

check_circle Halal
Permissible. Games generally free from prohibited elements, or where such elements are minor, incidental, completely avoidable, and easily disabled/modified without compromising the core experience. Primarily focuses on skill, strategy, problem-solving, or permissible themes.
warning Mashbouh
Doubtful / Caution Advised. Games containing elements whose permissibility is unclear, disputed among scholars, or contain problematic elements that may be avoidable but require significant player vigilance or effort. Engagement requires careful consideration and Taqwa (God-consciousness). Avoid if in doubt.
cancel Haram
Impermissible. Games featuring clear and unavoidable prohibited content or core mechanics integral to the gameplay that involve committing or simulating major sins, promoting vice, or containing themes fundamentally opposed to Islamic morality.
gpp_bad Kufr
Disbelief / Polytheism. Games containing explicit promotion, normalization, or requirement of participation in acts of Shirk (associating partners with Allah) or Kufr (disbelief), blasphemy, veneration of false deities, practice of magic (Sihr), or direct attacks on core Islamic tenets.

Severity Levels (for Breakdown Categories)

None
No significant concerns identified in this specific category based on review.
Low
Minor issues present, potentially avoidable, or requiring caution. May correspond to Mashbouh elements if pervasive.
Medium
Moderate concerns. Problematic elements are present and may be difficult to avoid or are normalized to some extent. Often found in Mashbouh or lower-end Haram ratings.
High
Severe concerns. Elements directly contradict Islamic principles and are likely integral or unavoidable. Usually indicates a Haram rating.
Prohibited
Explicitly prohibited content, often related to Kufr/Shirk or major unavoidable Haram actions. Indicates Haram or Kufr/Shirk rating.

Key Islamic Terms

Tawhid
The core Islamic concept of the absolute Oneness of Allah (God), central to faith.
Shirk
Associating partners with Allah; polytheism. The gravest sin in Islam.
Kufr
Disbelief or rejection of the fundamental tenets of Islam.
'Awrah
Parts of the body that must be covered according to Islamic guidelines. Exposure of 'Awrah without necessity is impermissible.
Zina
Unlawful sexual intercourse (fornication or adultery).
Qimar / Maysir
Gambling; games of chance involving stakes.
Laghw
Idle talk, vain pursuits, or activities that distract from religious duties or beneficial actions.
Akhlaq
Islamic ethics, morals, and manners.
As-Salaf As-Salih
The Pious Predecessors; refers to the first three generations of Muslims (Companions, Successors, Successors of the Successors), whose understanding of Islam is considered the most authentic.
